Weekly Current Affairs Digest: April 7–13, 2026

Curated, UPSC-filtered news with GS paper tagging, static-dynamic linkages, and answer framework hints for serious aspirants.

By The Governance Gazette Desk  ·  April 13, 2026  ·  7 min read

Illustration: The Governance Gazette

This is the weekly current affairs digest for the week of April 7–13, 2026. Each item is tagged with the relevant GS paper, linked to static syllabus topics, and accompanied by a potential Mains question angle.

1. Cabinet Approves Major Port Authorities Amendment Bill

The Union Cabinet approved the Major Port Authorities (Amendment) Bill, 2026, which empowers Major Port Authorities to lease port land for non-port activities and enter into public-private partnerships for port infrastructure development.

GS3 · Infrastructure · Economy

  • Static link: Major Port Authorities Act 2021, Sagarmala Programme, PPP model in infrastructure
  • Mains angle: "Examine the significance of port-led development in India's economic growth strategy."

2. Supreme Court Upholds PMLA Amendments

A five-judge Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court upheld the constitutional validity of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) amendments, affirming the Enforcement Directorate's powers to arrest, attach property, and seek bail reversals.

GS2 · Governance · Judiciary

  • Static link: PMLA 2002, ED's powers, Article 20 (protection against self-incrimination), right to bail
  • Mains angle: "The Supreme Court's upholding of PMLA amendments raises questions about the balance between anti-money laundering imperatives and civil liberties. Examine."

3. India Signs MoU with African Union on Digital Public Infrastructure

India and the African Union signed a Memorandum of Understanding on cooperation in Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI), covering UPI-based payment systems, digital identity, and the ONDC open network for digital commerce.

GS2 · Foreign Policy · GS3 · Digital Economy

  • Static link: India Stack, UPI, ONDC, India-Africa relations, South-South cooperation
  • Mains angle: "India's Digital Public Infrastructure has emerged as a key instrument of soft power. Analyse with examples."

4. RBI Announces Revised Liquidity Coverage Ratio Framework

The Reserve Bank of India issued revised guidelines on the Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) for scheduled commercial banks, requiring banks to maintain higher quality liquid assets to withstand a 30-day stress scenario.

GS3 · Economy · Banking

  • Static link: Basel III norms, LCR, NSFR, RBI as regulator, banking sector stability
  • Mains angle: "The RBI's revised LCR norms reflect lessons from global banking stress events. Examine the implications for Indian banks."

5. Draft Digital Competition Bill Open for Public Comment

The Ministry of Corporate Affairs released the Draft Digital Competition Bill, 2026, for public comment. The Bill proposes ex-ante regulation of "Systemically Significant Digital Enterprises" (SSDEs) — large tech platforms — to prevent anti-competitive behaviour before it causes market harm.

GS3 · Economy · Competition Law

  • Static link: Competition Act 2002, CCI, Big Tech regulation, Digital Markets Act (EU), data portability
  • Mains angle: "Ex-ante regulation of digital markets is preferable to ex-post enforcement. Do you agree? Examine in the context of India's Draft Digital Competition Bill."
